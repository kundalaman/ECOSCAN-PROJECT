Scientific Name: Dalbergia sissoo

Appearance:

Also known as Indian Rosewood, it is a medium to large deciduous tree with a straight trunk and distinctive, compound leaves. The wood is rich, dark brown to reddish-brown in color.

Medicinal Benefits:
  • Bark is used in traditional medicine to treat skin diseases, ulcers, and infections.
  • Leaves have anti-inflammatory properties and are used to relieve joint pain.
Cultural Significance in Indian Sanskriti:

Sheesham is valued for its strong wood, traditionally used to craft furniture and religious items. It is seen as auspicious and often used in temples and household altars. The tree is also mentioned in Ayurvedic texts for its healing benefits and represents strength and endurance.

Environmental Benefits:
  • Drought-resistant and helps prevent soil erosion through its deep-rooting system.
  • Supports biodiversity by offering habitat and food for wildlife.
  • Improves soil fertility by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
