Scientific Name: Ficus religiosa

Origin:

Native to the Indian subcontinent and widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

Type:

Large deciduous tree that can grow to 30 meters or more.

Leaves:

Heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with long petioles. They rustle in the wind and are easily recognizable.

Fruit:

Small, round figs that ripen to a purplish color. These are an important food source for birds and animals.

Trunk:

The Peepal tree often has a distinctive aerial root system, adding to its unique structure and support.

Medicinal Use:

Various parts of the tree are used to treat digestive disorders, respiratory issues, and skin conditions.

Cultural Significance:
  • Eternal Tree: Known as "Ashwattha" in Hindu mythology, symbolizing longevity and immortality.
  • Bodhi Tree: This tree is associated with the enlightenment of Gautama Buddha.
