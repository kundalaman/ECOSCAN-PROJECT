Scientific Name: Tectona grandis

Appearance:

It is a large, deciduous tree with broad, rough leaves and tall, straight trunks. The wood has a distinctive yellow-brown color that matures to a rich brown over time.

Medicinal Benefits:
  • The bark is traditionally used to treat headaches, fevers, and digestive issues.
  • Leaves have antibacterial properties and are used in folk medicine for skin treatments and wound healing.
Cultural Significance in Indian Sanskriti:

Teak wood has long been used in India for making high-quality furniture, doors, and construction, symbolizing wealth and prestige. Its strength and durability make it ideal for temple doors and religious artifacts. Teak forests are mentioned in traditional Indian texts as symbols of strength and heritage.

Environmental Benefits:
  • Excellent for reforestation and helps reduce soil erosion due to its large canopy.
  • Improves soil fertility as decomposing leaves return nutrients to the soil.
  • Supports biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various species.
