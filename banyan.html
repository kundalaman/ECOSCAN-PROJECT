Scientific Name: Ficus benghalensis

Origin:

Native to the Indian subcontinent and widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions across Asia.

Type:

A giant fig tree known for its immense size and sprawling canopy, offering shade to many organisms.

Aerial Roots:

Distinctive aerial roots grow from branches to the ground, forming secondary trunks and contributing to its unique structure.

Leaves:

Broad, elliptical, glossy green leaves provide dense foliage and shelter.

Fruit:

Small, round figs cluster along branches, serving as a vital food source for birds and mammals.

Shade and Shelter:

The Banyan tree's dense foliage and vast canopy offer shade and shelter to humans and animals, making it a beloved landmark in both rural and urban settings.

Sacred Symbolism:

The Banyan tree holds deep significance in Hindu mythology and is associated with deities like Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva. It represents longevity, wisdom, divine protection, and the interconnectedness of all life forms. The tree is considered eternal.
